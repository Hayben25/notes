## Basic Terms:

- What is misinformation?
	- False or misleading information that is *unknowingly* shared
- What is disinformation?
	- False or misleading information that is *purposely* created and distributed
- 3 kinds of lies
	- "There are 3 kinds of lies: lies, dammed lies, and statistics"

## Myth busters Quiz Answers

- The Myth Busters quiz answers are based on actual federal budget data from the fiscal budget from 2021

 1. Of the 4 following categories which does the federal government spend the most on?
	- police - in other category, unknown share of 4%
	- national defense - 13% of budget
	- Healthcare - 14% plus 12% for Medicare
	- veterans benefits - 4% of budget

	- "It just happens," \[Senator Bernie Sanders] said. "We don't worry about people sleeping out on the street"
2. Of the following categories of spending, which does the federal government spend the most on?
	- Interest on national debt - 9%
	- National defense - 13%
	- Social welfare spending 65% including healthcare, 39% excluding healthcare. This does not include veteran's benefits
	- Transportation - 2%

3. Of the following categories of spending, which does the federal government spend on?
	- customs and immigration - included in "other" less than 4%
	- healthcare - 27% including Medicare, 14% without Medicare
	- national defense - 13%
	- veteran's benefits - 4%

4. If we combine national defense spending and veteran's spending to create a category called "defense related spending", which of the following categories gets the largest share of federal spending?
	- Defense related spending - 17%
	- Social security - 21%
	- Medicare - 12%

5. Which of these gets the largest share of federal spending? Consider these categories:
	- defense related spending - 17%
	- Social security - 21%
	- Medicare - 12%
