**Definition**: Let $f(x,y)$ be continuous on the rectangle
- $R : a\le x\le b\ c\le y\le d$
- Let $P$ be a partition of $R$ and let $m_{ij}$ and $M_{ij}$ be the minimum and maximum values of $f$ on the $i,j$ sub-rectangle $R$ 
- $\therefore$
	- Lower sum: $$L_f(P)=\sum^n_{i=1}\sum^n_{j=1}m_{ij}\Delta x_i\Delta y_i$$
	- Upper sum: $$U_f(P)=\sum^n_{i=1}\sum^m_{j=1}M_{ij}\Delta x_i\Delta y_j$$
- The *double integral* of $f$ over $R$ is the unique number $I$ that satisfies $L_f(P)\le I\le U_f(P)$ for all partitions $P$
	- Notation: $$I=\iint_Rf(x,y)dxdy$$
	- Let $\Omega$ be an arbitrary closed bounded region in the plane then $$\iint_\Omega f(x,y)dxdy=\iint_RF(x,y)dxdy$$
	- Where $R$ is a rectangle that contains $\Omega$, and $F(x,y)=f(x,y)$ on $\Omega$ and $F(x,y)=0$ on $R-\Omega$
