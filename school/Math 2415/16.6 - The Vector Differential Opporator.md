$$\nabla=\frac{\partial}{\partial x}\hat{i}+\frac{\partial}{\partial y}\hat{j}+\frac{\partial}{\partial z}\hat{k}$$
- So we are familiar with $\nabla$ from our work with gradients but how does this work with vector fields?
	- If $f(x,y,z)$ is a scalar field $\nabla f(x,y,z)=(\frac{\partial}{\partial x},\frac{\partial}{\partial y},\frac{\partial}{\partial z})f(x,y,z)=(\frac{\partial f}{\partial x},\frac{\partial f}{\partial y},\frac{\partial f}{\partial z})$
	- How does this work with $\overrightarrow{v}(x,y,z)=(v_1(x,y,z),v_2(x,y,z),v_3(x,y,z))$ 
- The divergence of $\overrightarrow{v}$ is
	- $div\ \overrightarrow{v}=\nabla \overrightarrow v=)=(\frac{\partial v_1}{\partial x},\frac{\partial v_2}{\partial y},\frac{\partial v_3}{\partial z})=)=\frac{\partial v_1}{\partial x}+ \frac{\partial v_2}{\partial y}+ \frac{\partial v_3}{\partial z}$
- The cur of $\overrightarrow{v}$ is 
	- $curl\ \overrightarrow{v}=\nabla \times \overrightarrow{v}=\hat{i}(\frac{\partial v_3}{\partial y}-\frac{\partial v_2}{\partial z})-\hat{j}(\frac{\partial v_3}{\partial x}-\frac{\partial v_1}{\partial z})+\hat{k}(\frac{\partial v_2}{\partial x}-\frac{\partial v_1}{\partial y})$
	- curl of a gradient is 0
- The divergence of a curl is zero
