- $(x,y,z)\rightarrow(r,\theta,z)$ where
	- $x=rcos\theta$     $r^2=x^2+y^2$
	- $y=rsin\theta$     $tan\theta=\frac{y}{x}$
	- $z=z$
- $\iiint_\Omega f(x,y,z)dxdydz=\iiint_\tau F(r,\theta,z)drd\theta dz$
- $F(r,\theta,z)=f(rcos\theta,rsin\theta,z)$
## Examples

1. $$\int^\frac{2\pi}{3}_\frac{\pi}{4}\int_0^1\int_0^\sqrt{1-r^2}r\ dzdrd\theta=\int_\frac{\pi}{4}^\frac{2\pi}{3}d\theta\cdot\int_0^1\int_0^\sqrt{1-r^2}rdzdr=(\frac{8\pi-3\pi}{12})\int_0^1r\cdot\sqrt{1-r^2}dr=\frac{5\pi}{12}(-\frac{1}{2})\frac{2}{3}(1-r^2)^{3/2}=\frac{5\pi}{36}$$
2. Set up the integral to find volume of the solid bounded above by the hemisphere $z=\sqrt{4-x^2-y^2}$ and below by the cone $z=\sqrt{3x^2+3y^2}$
	- z-limits: $\sqrt{3x^2+3y^2}\le z\le\sqrt{4-x^2-y^2}$
	- xz plane $\sqrt{3}|x|\le z\le\sqrt{4-x^2}$
	- yz plane $\sqrt{3}|y|\le z\le\sqrt{4-y^2}$
	- To determine xy info where do we look? Projection of our solid onto xy-plane. Look at the "fattest" part of the solid
	- This is where $\sqrt{3x^2y^2}=\sqrt{4-x^2-y^2}$
		- $3x^2+3y^2=4-x^2-y^2$
		- $x^2+y^2=1$
	- $\Omega:-1\le x\le1,\ -\sqrt{1-x^2}\le y\le\sqrt{1-x^2}$
	- $$Vol=\int_{-1}^1\int_{-\sqrt{1-x^2}}^\sqrt{1-x^2}\int_\sqrt{3x^2+3y^2}^\sqrt{4-x^2-y^2} dzdydx$$
	- Looks like a lot of work, don't do this
	- z-limits: $\sqrt{3r^2}\le z\le\sqrt{4-r^2}$
		- $\sqrt{3}r\le z\le \sqrt{4-r^2}$
	- Polar: $0\le\theta\le2\pi,\ 1\le r\le 0$
	- $$Vol=\int_0^{2\pi}\int_0^1\int_{\sqrt{3}r}^\sqrt{4-r^2}r\ dzdrd\theta$$
