- $(x,y,z)\rightarrow (\rho,\theta,\phi)$
- $\rho^2=x^2+y^2+z^2=z^2+r^2$
- $x=\rho sin\phi cos\theta$
- $y=\rho sin\phi sin\theta$
- (notice: $r=\rho sin\phi$)
- $z=\rho cos\phi$ (so, $cos\phi=\frac{z}{\rho}$)
- Facts:
	- $\rho \ge 0$
	- $0\le \phi\le \pi$
		- $\phi =0$ +z-axis
		- $\phi =\pi$ -z-axis
	- $\theta$ is just regular $\theta$
- Triple Integrals $$\iiint_\tau f(x,y,z)dxdydz=\iiint_s f(\rho sin\phi cos\theta,\rho sin\phi cos\theta,\rho cos\phi)\rho^2sin\phi\ d\rho d\theta d\phi$$
	- Note: $dxdydz\rightarrow\rho^2 sin\phi\ d\rho d\theta d\phi$
	- Use when there is a spherical symmetry and you are integrating over a sphere or part of a sphere. The integrand will often involve $x^2+y^2+z^2$

## Examples

1. Evaluate the integral using sperical coordiantes $\iiint_\tau dxdydz$ where $\tau$ is the solid formed by $0\le x\le 1,\ 0\le y\le \sqrt{1-x^2}, \sqrt{x^2+y^2}\le z\le \sqrt{2-x^2-y^2}$
	- rz-plane $\sqrt{x^2+y^2}=r\le z\le \sqrt{2-x^2-y^2}=\sqrt{2-r^2}$
		- $r\le z\le\sqrt{2-r^2}$
		- $r=0\implies \rho sin\phi =0\implies sin\phi =0\implies \phi =0$
		- $r=z\implies \rho sin\phi =\rho cos\phi\implies \phi=\frac{\pi}{4}$
		- $r^2+z^2=2\implies \rho^2=2\implies \rho =\sqrt2$
	- For $\theta: 0\le x\le 1,\ 0\le y\le \sqrt{1-x^2}$
		- $0\le\theta\le\frac{\pi}{2}$
	- Spherical: $0\le\theta\le\frac{\pi}{3},\ 0\le\phi\le\frac{\pi}{4},\ 0\le\rho\le\sqrt2$$$Vol=\iiint_\tau dxdydz=\int_0^\frac{\pi}{2}\int_0^\frac{\pi}{4}\int_0^\sqrt2\rho^2sin\phi\ d\rho d\phi d\theta=\int_0^\frac{\pi}{2}d\theta\cdot\int_0^\frac{\pi}{4}sin\phi d\phi\cdot\int_0^\sqrt2\rho^2d\rho$$
2. Convert the integral into spherical coordinates $\iiint_\tau z\sqrt{x^2+y^2+z^2}dzdydx$ where $\tau$ is the solid formed by $0\le x\le\sqrt{4-y^2},\ 0\le y\le2,\ 0\le z\le\sqrt{4-x^2-y^2}$
	- rz-plane: $0\le z\le\sqrt{4-r^2}$
		- $\rho =2$
		- $0\le\phi\le\frac{\pi}{2}$
	- Spherical: $0\le\phi\le\frac{\pi}{2},\ 0\le\rho\le2$$$\int_0^\frac{\pi}{2}\int_0^\frac{\pi}{2}\int_0^2\rho^2cos\phi sin\phi\ d\rho d\phi d\theta$$
	- Cylindrical: $$\int_0^\frac{\pi}{2}\int_0^2\int_0^\sqrt{4-r^2}z\cdot r\sqrt{r^2+z^2}\ dzdrd\theta$$
3. Set up the integral to find volume of the solid bounded above by the hemisphere $z=\sqrt{4-x^2-y^2}$ and below by the cone $z=\sqrt{3x^2+3y^2}$
	- z-limits: $\sqrt{3x^2+3y^2}\le z\le\sqrt{4-x^2-y^2}$
		- $\sqrt3r\le z\le\sqrt{4-r^2}$
	- $r=0\implies\phi=0$
	- $z=\sqrt3r\implies\rho cos\phi=\sqrt3\rho sin\phi\implies\phi=\frac{\pi}{6}$
	- $r^2+z^2=4\implies\rho^2=4\implies\rho=2$
	- Spherical: $0\le\phi\le\frac{\pi}{6},\ 0\le\theta\le2\pi,\ 0\le\rho\le2$$$Vol=\int_0^{2\pi}\int_0^\frac{\pi}{6}\int_0^2\rho^2sin\phi\ d\rho d\phi d\theta$$
