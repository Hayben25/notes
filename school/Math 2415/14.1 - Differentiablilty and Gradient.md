- We say that $f$ is *differentiable* at **x** if there exist a vector **y** such that
	- $f(x+h)-f(x)=y\cdot h + o(h)$, $\overrightarrow{x}=(x,y)$,$\overrightarrow{h}=(h_1,h_2)$, $\overrightarrow{y}=(y_1,y_2)$
		- We use this instead of $lim_{h\to 0}\frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h}$ because we can't use division because h is a vector
- We will say that $g(h)$ is $o$(h) if $lim_{h\to 0}\frac{g(h)}{||h||}=0$
	- $lim_{h\to 0}\frac{g(h)}{||h||}=lim_{h\to 0}\frac{h_1^2}{\sqrt{h_1^2+h^2_2}}=0$
- Note from Calc 1:
	- $f'(x)=lim_{h\to 0}\frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h}$ means $f'(x)\approx \frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h}\implies f'(x)\cdot h\approx f(x+h)-f(x)$
- Let $f$ be differentiable at x. The *gradient of f* at x is the unique vector upside down $\Delta$f(x) such that
	- $f(x+h)-f(x)=\Delta f(x)\cdot h +o(h)$
	- $\Delta f(x)=(\frac{\partial f}{\partial x}, \frac{\partial f}{\partial y})$

## Example

1. For $f(x,y)=3x+y^2$  Let $\overrightarrow{x}=(x,y)$, $\overrightarrow{h}=(h_1,h_2)$, $\overrightarrow{h}$ is small
	- $f(\overrightarrow{x}+\overrightarrow{h})=f((x,y)+(h_1,h_2))=f(x+h_1,y+h_2)=3(x+h_1)+(y+h_2)^2=3x+3h_2+y^2+2yh_2+h_2^2$
	- $f(\overrightarrow{x}+\overrightarrow{h})-f(\overrightarrow{x})=3h_1+2yh_2+h_2^2=(3,2y)\cdot (h_1,h_2)+h_2^2$
	- Thus $\overrightarrow{y}=(3,2y)$
2. Find the graidient of $f(x,y)=2e^xsin(x^2+y)$
	- $f_x(x,y)=2e^xsin(x^2+y)+2xe^xcos(x^2+y)$
	- $f_y(x,y)=2e^xcos(x^2+y)$
	- $\Delta f(x,y)=(2e^xsin(x^2+y)+2xe^xcos(x^2+y), 2e^xcos(x^2+y))$
3. Find the gradient of $f(x,y,z)=xy-\sqrt{z}$ at (2,-1,4)
	- $f_x=y$
	- $f_y=x$
	- $f_z=-\frac{1}{2\sqrt{z}}$
	- $\Delta f(x,y,z)=(y,x,-\frac{1}{2\sqrt{z}})$
		- $\Delta f(2,-1,4)=(-1,2,-\frac{1}{4})$
