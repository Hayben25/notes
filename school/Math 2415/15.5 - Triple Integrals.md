Integration over an arbitrary solid: $$\iiint_Sf(x,y,z)dxdydz$$
- $dxdy=dA$
- $dxdydz=dV$
- $f(x,y,z)$
- Applications:
	1. "Volume" of a hypersolid $=\iiint_Sf(x,y,z)dxdydz$
	2. Volume of $S=\iiint_Sdxdydz$, $f(x,y,z)=1$
Reduction to a repeated integral

1. Type 1: $a\le x\le b$, $\phi_1\le y\le\phi_2(x)$, $\psi_1(x,y)\le z\le\psi_2(x,y)$
2. Type 2: $c\le y\le d$, $\phi_1(y)\le x\le\phi_2(y)$, $\psi_1(x,y)\le z\le\psi_2(x,y)$

## Examples

1. Calculate $\iiint_T zdxdydz$ where $T$ is the tetrahedron in the first octant bounded by the plane $x+y+z=1$
	- $T$ is bounded above by $x+y+z=1$ ($z=1-x-y$) and below by $xy$-plane ($z=0$)
		- So z-limits: $0\le z\le 1-x-y$
		- If $z=0\ in\ x+y+z=1\implies x+y=1$
	- $\Omega$ as Type 1: $0\le x\le1$, $0\le y\le 1-x$
	- $T$: $0\le x\le1$, $0\le y\le1-x$, $0\le z\le 1-x-y$
	- $$\iiint_Tzdxdydz=\int_0^1\int_0^{1-x}\int_0^{1-x-y}zdzdydx=\int_0^1\int_0^{1-x}(1-x-y)^2dydx$$
		- $u=1-x-y$, $du=-dy$, $-du=dy$
	- $$\frac{1}{2}\int_0^1(-1)(\frac{1}{3})(1-x-y)^3|^{1-x}_0dx=\frac{1}{6}\int_0^1(1-x)^3dx=\frac{1}{24}$$
2. Find the volume of the solid bounded above by the plane $y+z=2$, below by the $xy$-plane, and on the sides $x=6$, $y=\sqrt{x}$, $y=0$
	- z limits: $0\le z\le2-y$
	- $T$: $0\le y\le2$, $y^2\le x\le 6$, $0\le z\le2-y$
$$Vol=\iiint_Tdxdydz=\int_0^2\int_{y^2}^6\int_0^{2-y}dzdxdy=\int_0^2\int_{y^2}^6(2-y)dxdy=\int_0^2(2-y)(6-y^2)dy=\dots=\frac{32}{3}$$
