## Recall from Calc 1:

- $f'(x)=\lim_{h\to 0}\frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h}$
- If h is small, $f'(x)\approx \frac{f(x+h)-f(x)}{h}$
	- $f'(x)\cdot h\approx f(x+h)-f(x)$
		- $f'(x)\cdot h$ is the differential of f or $df$
		- $f(x+h)-f(x)$ is the increment of f or $\Delta f$

## Calc 3 now

- Now, for $f(x,y)$, the differential $df=\nabla f(x)\cdot h$ or
	- $df=\frac{\partial f}{\partial x}\Delta x+\frac{\partial f}{\partial y}\Delta y$ 
- When making **approximations**:
	- $f(x+\Delta x, y+\Delta y)\approx f(x,y)\cdot (\Delta x, \Delta y)$

## Examples

1. Find the differential $df$
	- $f(x,y,z)=xy+yz+xz$
	- $\nabla f(x,y,z)=(y+z,x+z,x+y)$
	- $df=\nabla f(x,y,z)\cdot \overrightarrow{h}$
		- $=(y+z,x+z,x+y)\cdot (\Delta x, \Delta y, \Delta z)$
		- $=(y+z)\Delta x +(x+z)\Delta y+(x+y)\Delta z$
2. Use differentials to approximate $\sqrt{125}(17)^{\frac{1}{4}}$
	- $f(x,y)=\sqrt{x}(y)^{\frac{1}{4}}$
	- We want $f(125,17)$, we know $f(121,16)=22$
		- $125=121+\Delta x\implies \Delta x=4$
		- $17=16+\Delta y\implies \Delta y=1$
		- $\overrightarrow{h}=(4,1)$
	- $\nabla f(x,y)=(\frac{y^{\frac{1}{4}}}{2x^\frac{1}{2}},\frac{x^{\frac{1}{2}}}{4y^{\frac{3}{4}}})$
	- $\nabla f(121,16)=(\frac{1}{11},\frac{11}{32})$
	- Now, $\sqrt{125}(17)^{1/4}=22+(1/11,11/32)\cdot(4,1)=22+\frac{249}{352}\approx 22.707$
