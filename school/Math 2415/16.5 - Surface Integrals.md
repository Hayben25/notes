- The total mass $M$, of a material surface with density function $\lambda$ is found by$$M=\iint_\Omega\lambda(x(u,v),y(u,v),z(u,v))||N(u,v)||dudv=\iint_S\lambda(x,y,z)d\sigma$$
- This calculation also holds for any scalar field $H$ which is continous over $S$ . We call this integral:$$\iint_SH(x,y,z)d\sigma=$$ The surface integral of $H$ over $S$
- If $H$ is 1 then we have $$\iint_Sd\sigma=$$ the area of $S$

## Example 

1. Evaluate $\iint_S2xyd\sigma$ over the surface $x+2y+2z=4$ in the first octant
	- What is H(x,y,z)?
		- 2xy
	- What is S
		- Triangle above the xy plane
	- Can we write z=f(x,y); yes
		- $z=\frac{1}{2}(4-x-2y)=f(x,y)$
		- $f_x=\frac{-1}{2}\ f_y=-1$
	- So $||N(x,y)||=\frac{3}{2}$
	- $\Omega$: $0\le x\le 4,\ 0\le y\le \frac{1}{2}(4-x)$
	- $$\iint_\Omega3xydydx=\int_0^4\int_0^{\frac{1}{2}(4-x)}3xydydx=8$$
