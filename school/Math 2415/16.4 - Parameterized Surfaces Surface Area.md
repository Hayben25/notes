## Parameterizing Surfaces:

1. A function $y=f(x)$ or $z=f(x,y)$
	- When $y=f(x),\ x\in[a,b]$
		- Let $x(u)=u,\ y(u)=f(u),\ u\in[a,b]$
			- $\overrightarrow{r}(u)=u\hat{i}+f(u)\hat{j}=(u,f(u))$
		- or shortcut
			- $\overrightarrow{r}(x)=(x,f(x))=x\hat{i}+f(x)\hat{j}\ x\in[a,b]$
	- If $z=f(x,y),\ (x,y)\in\Omega$
		- Let $x(u,v)= v\ y(u,v)=v$
			- $z(u,v)=f(x,y)$, $(u,v)\in\Omega$
			- $\overrightarrow{r}(u,v)=u\hat{i}+v\hat{j}+f(u,v)\hat{k}=(u,v,f(u,v))\ (u,v)\in\Omega$
		- or shortcut
			- $\overrightarrow{r}(x,y)=x\hat{i}+y\hat{j}+f(x,y)\hat{k}=(x,y,f(x,y))\ (x,y)\in\Omega$
2. A plane:
	- $A(x-x_0)+B(y-y_0)+C(z-z_0)=0$ or $Ax+By+Cz=D$
	- Solve for $z=\frac{1}{C}(D-Ax-By)=f(x,y)$
		- use $\overrightarrow{r}(u,v)=u\hat{i}+v\hat{j}+\frac{1}{C}(D-Au-Bv)\hat{k}=(u,v,\frac{1}{C}(D-Au-Bv))$
3. A sphere
	- $x^2+y^2+z^2=a^2$
		- We cannot solve for z!
	- Recall:  $x^2+y^2=a^2$
		- use $x=acos(u),\ y=asin(u),\ u\in[0,2\pi]$
	- $x^2+y^2+z^2=a^2$
		- Let $x(u,v)=acos(u)cos(v),\ y(u,v)=asin(u)cos(v),\ z(u,v)=a\cdot sin(v)$
			- $u\in[0,2\pi],\ v\in[-\frac{\pi}{2},\frac{\pi}{2}]$
- Let $S$ be a surface parameterized by $\overrightarrow{r}(u,v)=x(u,v)\hat{i}+y(u,v)\hat{j}+z(u,v)\hat{k}$. The cross Product $(\overrightarrow{N})=\overrightarrow{r}_u'\times\overrightarrow{r}_v'$
	- $\overrightarrow{N}$ is normal to the surface
- $S$ is a **smooth surface** if it is parameterized by a continuous differentiable function $\overrightarrow{r}(u,v)$ with $(u,v)$ in $\Omega$. We will also assume that the fundamental vector product is never zero on the interior of $\Omega$ 
	- The area of $S$ is given by: $\iint_\Omega||N(u,v)||dudv$
		- Think of finding arc length
- If we have $\Omega$ as a projection of a surface $S$ on the $xy$-plane, then we have $S$ is the graph of the function $z=f(x,y)$ with $(x,y)$ in $\Omega$ and then:
	- The area of $S$ is given by: $\iint_\Omega\sqrt{[f_x(x,y)]^2+[f_y(x,y)]^2+1}dxdy$

## Example

1. Find the fundamental vector product for $\overrightarrow{r}(u,v)=cos(u)sin(v)\hat{i}+sinucosv\hat{j}+u\hat{k}$
	- $x=cos(u)sin(v),\ y=sin(u)cos(v),\ z=u$
		- $\overrightarrow{r_u}(u,v)=(-sin(u)sin(v),cos(u)\cdot cos(v),1)$
		- $\overrightarrow{r_v}(u,v)=(cos(u)cos(v),sin(u)\cdot sin(v),0)$
	- $\overrightarrow{N}(u,v)=(sin(u)sin(v),cos(u)cos(v),sin^2(u)sin^2(v)-cos^2(u)cos^2(v))$ 
2. Find the area of the surface: $3z^2=(x+y)^3,\ x+y\le 2,x\ge0,\ y\ge0,z\ge0$ 
	- Can we find $z=f(x,y)$?
		- Yes since $z\ge0$
	- $z=\sqrt{\frac{1}{3}(x+y)^3}=\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}(x+y)^\frac{3}{2}=f(x,y)$
		- $f_x=\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\frac{3}{2}(x+y)^\frac{1}{2}=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}(x+y)^\frac{1}{2}$
		- $f_y=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}(x+y)^\frac{1}{2}$
	- $\Omega: 0\le x\le 2, 0\le y\le 2-x$
