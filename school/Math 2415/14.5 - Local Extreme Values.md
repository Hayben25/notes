## Definitions and Theorems

- Definition 1. (Local max. and min.) Let $f$ be a funciton of several variables and let $x_0$ be an interior point of the domain:
	- $f$ has a *local maximum* at $x_0$ if
		- $f(x_0)\ge f(x)$ for all $x$ in some neighborhood of $x_0$
	- $f$ has a *local minimum* at $x_0$ if
		- $f(x_0)\le f(x)$ for all $x$ in some neighborhood of $x_0$
	- As in the one-variable case, the local maxima and minima together are the *local extreme values*
- Theorem 1. If $f$ takes on a local extreme values at $x_0$, then either $\nabla f(x_0)=0$ or $\nabla f(x_0)$ DNE
	- CRITICAL POINTS (lol)
	- Critical points at which the gradient is zero are  *stationary points*. The stationary points that do not give rise to local extreme values are called *saddled points* 
- Theorem 2. (The Second-Partials Test) Suppose that $\nabla f(x_0,y_0)=0$ and that $f$ has continuous second-order partial derivatives in a neighborhood of (x_0,y_0). Set $A=\frac{\partial ^2f}{\partial x^2}(x_0,y_0)$, $B=\frac{\partial ^2f}{\partial x\partial y}(x_0,y_0)$, $C=\frac{\partial ^2f}{\partial y^2}(x_0,y_0)$ and form the discriminant $D=AC-B^2$
- If $D\lt 0$, then $(x_0,y_0)$ is a saddle point
- If $D\gt 0$, then $f$ takes on
	- a local min @ $(x_0,y_0)$ if $A\gt 0$
	- a local max @ $(x_0,y_0)$ if $A\lt 0$
- If $D=0$ inconclusive
## Examples

1. Find the stationary points and local extreme values.
	- $f(x,y)=x^2+2xy+3y^2+2x+10y+1$
	- Lone stationary point is $P(1,-2)$
	- $A=2$, $B=2$, $C=6$
	- Since $D \gt 0$ check $A$
		- $A\gt 0$
	- $\therefore f(1,-2)=-8$ is a local min
