Given $F=F(r,\theta)$ continuous on
- $\tau:\ a\le r\le b,\alpha\le\theta\le\beta$
- $$\iint_\tau F(r,\theta)r\ drd\theta=\int_\alpha^\beta\int_a^bF(r,\theta)r\ drd\theta$$
And the double integral of $F(r,\theta)$ over the polar region

- $\Omega:\alpha\le\theta\le\beta$, $\rho_1(\theta)\le r\le \rho_2(\theta)$
$$\iint_\Omega F(r,\theta)r\ drd\theta=\int^\beta_\alpha\int_{\rho_1(\theta)}^{\rho_2(\theta)}F(r,\theta)r\ drd\theta$$

| Rectangular                 | Polar                                                  |
| --------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ |
| $A=\iint_\Omega dxdy$       | $A=\int_\alpha^\beta\int_a^br\ drd\theta$              |
| $V=\iint_\Omega f(x,y)dxdy$ | $V=\int_\alpha^\beta\int_a^b F(r,\theta)r\ drd\theta$  |
In general $f(x,y)=f(rcos(\theta),rsin(\theta))=F(r,\theta)$
