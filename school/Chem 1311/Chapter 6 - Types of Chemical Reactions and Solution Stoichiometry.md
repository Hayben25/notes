## 6.1.1 Solution Stoichiometry

- Concentration of solutions - amount of solute in a given volume of solution
- Molarity (M) or molar concentration - common unit
	- $M=\frac{n_{solute}}{V_{solution}\ (L)}$ or $M=\frac{moles\ of\ solute}{L\ of\ solution}$
- Ex. 500 ml containing 0.730 mol glucose
	- Molar concentration?
		- $M=\frac{0.730\ mol}{0.500\ L}=1.46\ m/L=1.46\ M\implies$ don't actually need 1 L
- M refers to what is originally dissolved $\implies$ does not take into account subsequent processes
	- Ex. $Ba(NO_3)_2\ (s)\rightarrow Ba^{2+}\ (aq)+2NO_3^-\ (aq)$ in $H_2O$
		- 1 M barium nitrate solution
			- $\implies [Ba^{2+}]=1M$  and $[NO_3^-]=2M$
				- $[\dots]$ - concentration
	- Ex. How many grams of $Na_2SO_4$ are needed to prepare 250 ml of a 0.683 M solution
		- n=MV
		- $n_{Na_2So_4}=V*M= (0.250\ L)*(0.683\ M\ Na_2SO_4)=0.171\ mol\ Na_2SO_4$
		- $g\ Na_2SO_4=0.171\ mol * 142.1\ g/mol=24.3\ g$
- Dilution of Solutions
	- Common to have a *stock solution*
		- Concentrated solution that can be diluted to the concentration desired
	- Dilution - adding solvent to decrease the concentration
		- $n_{solute}$ before dilution $=n_{solute}$ after dilution
			- $n_{solute}=M*V$
		- $M_{initial}*V_{initial}=M_{final}*V_{final}$
	- Ex. How prepare $5.00 * 10^2\ ml$ of a 1.75 M $HNO_3$ solution from 8.61 M stock solution of $HNO_3$
		- $M_{final}=1.75\ M$, $M_{initial}=8.61\ M$, $V_{final}=0.500\ L$
		- $M_{initial}*V_{initial}=M_{final}*V_{final}\implies V_{initial}=\frac{M_{final}*V_{final}}{M_{initial}}=\frac{1.75\ M* .5\ L}{8.61\ M}=1.02\ L$
	- Can carry out stoichiometric calculations
		- What volume of 1.50 M $Pb(CH_3COO)_2$ contains .4 mol Pb$^{2+}$?
			- $Pb(CH_3COO)_2\rightarrow Pb^{2+}+2CH_3COO^-$
			- $n_{Pb^{2+}}=n_{Pb(AC)_2}=M\times V\implies V=\frac{n_{Pb^{2+}}}{M}=\frac{.4\ mol}{1.5\ M}=0.267\ L=267\ ml$
		- How many grams of solid PbCl$_2$ forms when this volume reacts with 125 ml of 3.4 M NaCl?
			- $Pb(CH_3OO)_2(aq)+2NaCl(aq)\rightarrow PbCl_2(s)+2NaCH_3COO(aq)$
			- $n_{NaCl}=M*V=(0.125\ L)*(3.4\ M)=0.425\ mol$
				- $n_{PbCl_2}=n_{NaCl}*\frac{1\ mol\ PbCl_2}{2\ mol\ NaCl}=0.213$
			- $n_{Pb^{2+}}=0.400\ mol$
				- If $Pb^{2+}$ limiting reagent $\rightarrow$ 0.400 mol
			- NaCl is the limiting reagent
			- $g_{PbCl_2}=n_{PbCl_2}*M_{PbCl_2}=0.213\ mol * 278.1\ g/mol=59.1\ g$

## 6.3 Acid/Base Reaction

- Br√∏nsted-Lowry: (previous Arrhenius Definition)
	- Acid: proton donor (See pg 540,550)
	- Base: proton acceptor Strong (memorize)
- Most common weak bases
	- $NH_3$, $NH_2-R$, (R= alkyl group)
	- $NH_2-R$ is an amine
- Generally in $H_2O$
	- $acid +base\rightarrow salt+water$
		- \[acid-base or neutralization reaction]
	- strong acid + strong base
		- $HCl(aq)+NaOH(aq)\rightarrow NaCl(aq)+H_2O(l)$
	- Strong acids and bases completely ionized
	- Total ionic equation:
		- $H^+(aq)+Cl^-+Na^+(aq)+OH^-(aq)\rightarrow Na^+(aq)+Cl^-(aq)+H_2O(l)$
	- Net ionic equaiton
		- $H^+(aq)+OH^-(aq)\rightarrow H_2O(l)$
		- 1 mol HCl $\rightarrow$ 1 mol H$^+$
		- 1 mol NaOH $\rightarrow$ 1 mol OH$^-$
	- When $n_{HCl}=n_{NaOH}$ at the end of reaction would have no leftover acid or base (they are neutralized)
- Weak acid ($CH_3COOH$ & $NaOH$)
	- $OH^-(aq)+CH_3COOH(aq)\rightarrow CH_3COO^-(aq)+H_2O(l)$
	- $H^+$ moves (proton-transfer)
- What volume of 0.01060 M HBr (aq) is needed to neutralize 25.00 ml of 0.01580 M Ba(OH)$_2$?
	- $2HBr(aq)+Ba(OH)_2(aq)\rightarrow BaBr_2(aq)+2H_2O$
	- $2(M_{Ba(OH)_2}\times V_{Ba(OH)_2})=M_{HBr}\times V_{HBr}\implies \frac{2(M_{Ba(OH)_2}\times V_{Ba(OH)_2})}{M_{HBr}}=\frac{2*.025*.0158}{.0106}=.07453\ L=74.53\ ml$

## 6.5 Oxidation-Reduction Redox Reactions

- E.x. $2Ca(s)+O_2(g)\rightarrow 2CaO(s)$
	- ionic compound: $Ca^{2+}$,$O^{2-}$
	- Half reactions
		- $2Ca \rightarrow 2Ca^{2+}+4e^-$ (oxidation - loss of e$^-$)
		- $O_2+4e^-\rightarrow 2O^{2-}$ (reduction - gain of e$^-$)
		- Both reactions occur at the same time
		- $O_2$ = oxidizing agent - causes the other species (Ca) to be oxidized (it is itself reduced)
- E.x. Write the $\frac{1}{2}$ reactions, identify the oxidizing and reducing agents for $Ba+F_2\rightarrow BaF_2$
	- Oxidizing agent - $F_2$
	- Reducing agent - $Ba$
	- Half reactions
		- $Ba \rightarrow Ba^{2+}+2e^-$ (oxidation - loss of e$^-$)
		- $F_2+2e^-\rightarrow 2F^{-}$ (reduction - gain of e$^-$)
- Redox reaction also occur hen e$^-$ density shifts (covalent compounds)
- E.x. $H_2(g)+Cl_2(g)\rightarrow 2HCl(g)$
	- Fig. question 3.18
	- e$^-$ density distorted towards the Cl and away from the H
