4. A spherical tank stores 10 000 gallons of gasoline. If the tank walls are 10 cm thick, what is the outer diameter of the spherical tank in meters? What is the volume of material needed to fabricate the tank?
	- Outer Diameter:
		- 1 gal = 3785.4 cm$^3$
		- $10\ 000\times\frac{3785.4\ cm^3}{1\ gal}\approx 37\ 854\ 000\ cm^3$
		- $V=\frac{4}{3}\pi r^3$
			- $\implies 37\ 854\ 000\ cm^3=\frac{4}{3}\pi r^3$
			- $\implies r\approx 208.293\ cm$
		- $d_{innner}=2r\approx 416.586\ cm$
		- $d_{outer}=d_{inner}+2\times (10\ cm)\approx 436.586\ cm \times \frac{1\ m}{100\ cm}=4.36586\ m$
	- Volume of materials for fabrication:
		- $V_{outer}-V_{inner}=V_{fabrication}$
		- $V_{inner}=10\ 000\ gal=37\ 854\ 000\ cm^3$
		- $V_{outer}=\frac{4}{3}\pi r_{outer}^3=\frac{4}{3}\pi (\frac{d_{outer}}{2})^3=\frac{4}{3}\pi(\frac{436.586\ cm}{2})^3\approx 43\ 571\ 972.42\ cm^3$
		- $V_{fabrication}=43\ 571\ 972.42\ cm^3 - 37\ 854\ 000\ cm^3\approx 5\ 717\ 972.42\ cm^3\times \frac{1\ m^3}{1\ 000\ 000\ cm^3}=5.70\ m^3$
